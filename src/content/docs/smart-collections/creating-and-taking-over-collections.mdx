Smart Collections can manage a collection in two ways: create a new one from scratch, or take over an existing collection already in your store. Both result in a collection whose membership and sort order are controlled automatically.

## Setting up a collection

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Create new">

When you create a collection through Smart Collections, it creates a matching Shopify collection and immediately takes ownership of its membership and sorting.

<Steps>

1. Click **New Collection** in the Smart Collections dashboard
2. Select **Create new collection**
3. Enter a title — this becomes the Shopify collection title. Optionally add a description, SEO title, SEO description, and handle.
4. Configure your membership rules (see [Rule-Based Collections](/smart-collections/rule-based-collections/))
5. Click **Save and sync**

</Steps>

The collection appears in your Shopify store immediately. Smart Collections runs an initial sync to populate it based on your rules.

:::note
After creation, Shopify owns the collection's title, description, image, SEO fields, and handle. You can edit any of these from your Shopify admin or from the collection's detail page in Smart Collections. Smart Collections only controls which products are in it and their order.
:::

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="Take over existing">

You can hand off an existing manual Shopify collection to Smart Collections without disrupting it. The collection stays live in your store throughout the process.

:::note
Only manual Shopify collections can be taken over. Shopify automated collections (those with Shopify-managed rules) are not supported — Smart Collections will reject them because Shopify's rule engine conflicts with its own membership management. To migrate a Shopify automated collection, create a new Smart Collections collection with equivalent rules instead.
:::

### Choose a takeover mode

When taking over an existing collection, you choose one of two modes:

| Mode | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Adopt** | Preserves all existing products as a baseline. Your rules take effect alongside the baseline over time. The first sync makes **zero changes** to your storefront. A baseline import runs automatically before activation. |
| **Enforce** | Applies your rules immediately. Products that do not match are removed on the first sync. No baseline import is required — the collection is ready to activate immediately after setup. |

**Adopt mode is recommended** for any live collection your customers are actively browsing. It gives you time to configure and validate rules without risking unexpected changes to your storefront.

### Setting up a takeover

<Steps>

1. Click **New Collection** in the Smart Collections dashboard
2. Select **Take over existing collection**
3. Search for the collection by name and select it
4. Choose a takeover mode — **Adopt** or **Enforce**
5. Configure your membership rules
6. Click **Save and sync**

</Steps>

### What happens in Adopt mode

When you take over in Adopt mode, Smart Collections runs a **baseline import** before the collection can be activated:

<Steps>

1. Smart Collections reads the collection's current product list from Shopify — this is read-only and makes no changes to your store
2. Every product in the collection is recorded as the baseline
3. The collection becomes activatable once the baseline import completes

</Steps>

![Collection detail page showing the baseline import progress indicator with a spinner and "Importing baseline…" message](/screenshots/placeholder.png)

The progress indicator on the collection detail page tracks the import. For most collections this completes within seconds; large collections may take a minute or two.

:::note
**The collection cannot activate until the baseline import is complete.** This is intentional — it ensures the first sync produces zero changes. Once the import finishes, the collection transitions from Pending to Active automatically.
:::

### The first activation guarantee

Once the baseline import completes and you activate the collection, the first sync produces **zero changes** — no products are added and no products are removed. Your storefront looks exactly as it did before Smart Collections took ownership.

This guarantee holds regardless of what rules you have configured, because Adopt mode inserts a special condition that matches every product in your original baseline.

:::note
When you open the Sync Dashboard after your first activation, you will see this sync recorded with **0 products added** and **0 products removed**. This is the expected and correct result — it confirms the guarantee held and your collection was preserved exactly.
:::

### Evolving your rules over time

After takeover, your collection detail page shows the baseline as a read-only **"Original collection products"** item in the rules panel. This is not a rule you can edit directly — it is the safety net that preserves your original products.

Over time, you can:

- **Add rules** — conditions you add narrow the baseline or expand it with OR logic
- **Add nested groups** — combine AND and OR conditions to express complex merchandising logic
- **Remove the baseline** — when you are ready for rules to fully control membership, remove the baseline via **Danger Zone → Remove baseline**

### Remove baseline

When you remove the baseline, only products matching your authored rules remain in the collection. Products that were in the collection only because of the baseline are removed on the next sync.

:::note
Removing the baseline does **not** delete the snapshot. Your original product set is still recorded, so you can always restore it later.
:::

### Restore baseline

If you have removed the baseline and want to reinstate it, use **Restore baseline** on the collection detail page. This re-adds the original baseline condition — Smart Collections re-reads the stored snapshot and applies it on the next sync.

### If the baseline import fails

If the baseline import encounters an error (for example, a temporary Shopify API issue), the collection detail page shows a failure notice with a **Retry import** button. Click it to restart the import. The import is safe to retry — it always reads the latest state from Shopify and records it cleanly regardless of whether a partial import had run before.

The collection remains non-activatable until a retry succeeds.

### What changes when you take over a collection

| Area | Before takeover | After takeover |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Membership | Managed manually | Managed by Smart Collections rules |
| Sort order | Shopify-controlled | Smart Collections-controlled (set to Manual in Shopify) |
| Title, description, image, SEO | Shopify-owned | Shopify-owned (unchanged) |
| Automatic updates | None | Automatic sync on product changes |

## The collection detail page

Once a collection is managed, its detail page shows:

- Current sync status and last sync time
- Rule configuration
- Sort configuration and pin settings
- Sync history (last 10 syncs, with products added, removed, and reordered)

A **Sync now** button lets you trigger an immediate re-evaluation and re-sort at any time.

## Pausing and resuming automation

You can pause automation on a collection without releasing it or losing any configuration. This is useful when you are making significant changes to your catalogue or rules and want to prevent syncs from running in the interim.

### Pause automation

<Steps>

1. Open the collection in Smart Collections
2. Click **Pause automation** in the collection detail header
3. Confirm — the collection status changes to **Paused** immediately

</Steps>

While paused:

- Collection membership and sorting are not automatically synchronised while paused, including webhook-triggered evaluations
- Rules and sort settings can still be configured and saved
- The collection remains visible in your Shopify store with its last-synced product set intact
- The collection appears as **Paused** in your collections list

### Reactivate automation

<Steps>

1. Open the paused collection in Smart Collections
2. Click **Reactivate automation**
3. Smart Collections queues a full evaluation sync as soon as automation is reactivated

</Steps>

:::tip
Pausing is ideal when you are restructuring a category, expecting a large catalogue import, or troubleshooting unexpected membership changes. Because configuration is retained, you can pick up exactly where you left off.
:::

## Stopping management

### Release a collection

Releasing a collection stops Smart Collections from managing it without deleting it from Shopify. The collection remains in your store with its current products and positions intact.

:::note
Releasing restores the sort order that was active when you took over. Only the sort mode changes — products are not moved or deleted.
:::

<Steps>

1. Open the collection in Smart Collections
2. Scroll to the **Danger Zone** section
3. Click **Stop managing collection**
4. Confirm in the dialog

</Steps>

After release, the collection returns to full merchant control. You can take it over again at any time.

:::caution
Only the store owner can release or delete a collection. Staff members with Shopify admin access can manage rules, sort settings, and syncs, but cannot perform these destructive actions.
:::

### Delete a collection

Deleting a collection permanently removes it from Shopify.

<Steps>

1. Open the collection in Smart Collections
2. Scroll to the **Danger Zone** section
3. Click **Delete collection**
4. Confirm in the dialog

</Steps>

:::caution
This permanently removes the Shopify collection and cannot be undone. Sync history is preserved in Smart Collections for reference, but the collection and all its storefront associations are gone.

If you only want to stop automating a collection without removing it, use **Stop managing collection** instead.
:::

## If a collection is deleted in Shopify admin

If a managed collection is deleted from Shopify admin rather than through Smart Collections, the app typically detects this via a webhook and removes it from your dashboard. Sync history is preserved.

If the collection still shows an error status after being deleted in Shopify, release it from the Danger Zone — Smart Collections detects the collection no longer exists and cleans up without error.

## General

<details open>
  <summary>Does Smart Collections work with all Shopify themes?</summary>

Yes. Smart Collections manages collection membership and sort order through the Shopify API. It does not modify your theme files or require any theme customisation. Your storefront displays whatever products Shopify has in each collection — Smart Collections controls what those are.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Will Smart Collections affect my collections that I have not connected to it?</summary>

No. Smart Collections only manages collections you explicitly connect to it, either by creating a new collection through the app or by taking over an existing one. All other Shopify collections — automated or manual — are untouched.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Can I use Smart Collections alongside Shopify's built-in automated collections?</summary>

Yes. Smart Collections–managed collections and native Shopify automated collections can coexist in the same store without conflict.

However, you cannot take over a Shopify automated collection directly — Smart Collections will reject it because Shopify's own rule engine would conflict with its membership management. To migrate a Shopify automated collection, recreate it as a new Smart Collections collection with equivalent rules, then delete or repurpose the original.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>What permissions does Smart Collections use?</summary>

Smart Collections uses the **Products** permission (read and write) to manage your collection membership and sort order. It does not access theme files, customer records, or order data. GDPR compliance webhooks are registered as required by Shopify, but the app stores no customer personal data.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Who can manage collections?</summary>

Any Shopify admin user on your store can create collections, take over existing collections, edit rules and sort settings, trigger manual syncs, and edit collection content fields.

Only the **store owner** can release a collection or delete a collection through Smart Collections.

</details>

## Collection Takeover & Baseline

<details open>
  <summary>What happens to my existing products when I take over a collection?</summary>

It depends on the mode you choose:

- **Adopt mode** (recommended) — Smart Collections takes a snapshot of the current products and saves them as a "baseline". The first activation makes zero changes to your storefront. Your existing products remain in the collection until your rules would naturally remove them. You can evolve your rules gradually over time.
- **Enforce mode** — Smart Collections evaluates your rules immediately. Products that do not match are removed on the first sync.

See [Creating & Taking Over Collections](/smart-collections/creating-and-taking-over-collections/) for the full explanation and a comparison of both modes.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>What is the baseline and why does it exist?</summary>

The baseline is a record of every product that was in your collection at the moment Smart Collections took it over in Adopt mode. It is used to guarantee that the first activation makes no changes to your storefront.

After takeover, the baseline products appear in your rule builder as a read-only **"Original collection products"** condition. They are treated as rule members until you remove the baseline.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Can I remove the baseline once I have set up my rules?</summary>

Yes. When you are ready for your rules to fully control membership — and you no longer want the original products held in the collection automatically — go to **Danger Zone → Remove baseline** on the collection detail page.

This does not delete the underlying snapshot. If you later want to restore it, use **Restore baseline** — the snapshot is always preserved.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>What should I do if the baseline import is stuck or failed?</summary>

Open the collection detail page. If the import is in PENDING with no progress after 10 minutes, click **Retry import**. If it shows FAILED, the same button appears with an error message explaining the cause.

See [Troubleshooting — Baseline import is stuck or failed](/smart-collections/troubleshooting/#baseline-import-is-stuck-or-failed) for step-by-step guidance.

</details>

## Rules & Membership

<details>
  <summary>How quickly does a collection update after a product change?</summary>

Typically within a few seconds. When a product is created, updated, or deleted in your Shopify store, Smart Collections receives a webhook and re-evaluates all affected collections automatically. For stores with large catalogues, evaluation may take a little longer.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>What happens to a product if it stops matching the collection rules?</summary>

It is removed from the collection on the next sync. The product itself is not affected in any way — only its membership in that specific collection changes. It will be re-added automatically if it later changes to match the rules again.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Can I combine AND and OR logic in the same rule?</summary>

Yes. The rule builder supports nested condition groups. Use **Add group** to insert a new nested group, or **Wrap in group** to convert an existing condition into a group.

Each group has its own AND/OR toggle, letting you express logic like "products tagged 'sale' under £100, OR products tagged 'clearance' with fewer than 5 in stock". See [Rule-Based Collections — Nested AND/OR groups](/smart-collections/rule-based-collections/#nested-andor-groups).

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Can I force a product to always be included, even if it does not match the rules?</summary>

Per-product overrides are a planned feature. In the current version, all products are included or excluded based on rule evaluation. Pinning a product to the top or bottom only controls position for products that are already members.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Why does my live product count differ from what ends up in the collection?</summary>

The live count is backed by a cached snapshot of your catalogue that refreshes every 5 minutes, so it can be slightly behind your live store. The actual sync always uses the current state of each product at the moment it runs — so if products changed between when you viewed the count and when the sync ran, the final membership may differ.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Does Smart Collections evaluate draft products?</summary>

Draft products are included in evaluation and can appear in a collection if your rules do not exclude them. To exclude draft products, add a condition `Status = Active` to your rules.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>What is the difference between a rule-based collection and a composed collection?</summary>

A **rule-based collection** evaluates your full product catalogue against conditions you define.

A **composed collection** takes its membership from other Smart Collections–managed collections. You can optionally add local rule conditions to filter that inherited product set further.

See [Rule-Based Collections](/smart-collections/rule-based-collections/) and [Composed Collections](/smart-collections/composed-collections/) for details.

</details>

## Activity & Visibility

<details>
  <summary>Where can I see a history of configuration changes?</summary>

Open **Activity** in the main navigation. The Activity page shows every rule change, sort change, lifecycle event, and Shopify Admin edit across all your managed collections, with actor attribution and change summaries. Each collection's detail page also shows a collection-specific timeline.

See [Activity](/smart-collections/activity/) for full details.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>How long is activity history kept?</summary>

Activity events are retained for one year. Sync run change detail (which products were added, removed, or reordered) is retained for 90 days. See [Troubleshooting — Data retention](/smart-collections/troubleshooting/#data-retention).

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Can I see when someone changed a rule or paused automation?</summary>

Yes. Every configuration change, lifecycle event, and externally detected Shopify Admin change is recorded in the Activity timeline with the actor (Merchant, Shopify Admin, Smart Collections, or System) and a timestamp. Rule changes include condition-level summaries showing exactly what was added or removed.

</details>

## Sales channels

<details>
  <summary>Can I control which sales channels a collection is published to from Smart Collections?</summary>

Yes. Each collection detail page has a **Sales channels** section in the right panel. Check or uncheck channels, then click **Save** — the same save that commits rule and sort changes. Smart Collections applies the publication changes to Shopify immediately.

See [Sales Channel Management](/smart-collections/sales-channel-management/) for the full workflow.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Does Smart Collections change which channels a collection is published to automatically?</summary>

No. Smart Collections never changes publication state on its own. It applies publication changes only when you explicitly save them from the collection detail page. It does, however, detect when you change publication state in Shopify Admin directly, and records that change in Activity.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Will publication changes appear in Activity?</summary>

Yes. Every publication change is recorded as a **Sales channels changed** event in Activity — whether it was made through Smart Collections (actor: Merchant) or directly in Shopify Admin (actor: Shopify Admin). Each event includes a summary of which channels were added or removed.

</details>

## Sync & Monitoring

<details>
  <summary>Where can I see all my sync activity?</summary>

Open the **Sync Dashboard** via **Sync** in the main navigation. It shows all syncs currently in progress across every collection (Active Syncs), plus a searchable, filterable history of completed and failed syncs (Sync History).

See [Sync Dashboard](/smart-collections/sync-dashboard/) for full details.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>My first sync after taking over a collection shows 0 products added and 0 removed. Is something wrong?</summary>

No — this is the expected result when you take over a collection in **Adopt mode**. It confirms that the first-activation guarantee held: Smart Collections preserved your collection exactly as it existed before takeover. No products were added or removed.

See [Creating & Taking Over Collections — The first activation guarantee](/smart-collections/creating-and-taking-over-collections/#the-first-activation-guarantee) for a full explanation.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>A sync is showing as failed. What should I do?</summary>

Open the [Sync Dashboard](/smart-collections/sync-dashboard/) and find the failed run in Sync History. Click the row to expand it — the error message and step detail are shown inline. Click **View collection →** to navigate directly to the collection and trigger a manual retry via **Sync now**.

If failures repeat across multiple retries, see [Troubleshooting](/smart-collections/troubleshooting/) for common failure patterns.

</details>

## Sorting & Pins

<details>
  <summary>Can I pin products and still use automated sorting?</summary>

Yes. Top-pinned products are placed first, bottom-pinned products are placed last, and the automated sort applies to everything in between. See [Product Pins](/smart-collections/product-pins/).

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Does Smart Collections override the sort order I set in Shopify admin?</summary>

Yes. When a collection is managed by Smart Collections, it sets Shopify's sort mode to Manual and controls all product positions. If you change the sort mode or reorder products in Shopify admin, Smart Collections will restore its configuration on the next sync.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>What does out-of-stock demotion do exactly?</summary>

When enabled, any product with zero total inventory is moved to the end of the collection, after all in-stock products are sorted. Pinned products are exempt and stay in their pinned positions regardless of stock level.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Will pinned products be removed if they stop matching the rules?</summary>

Yes. A pin controls position, not membership. If a pinned product no longer satisfies the collection's rules, Smart Collections removes it from the collection. If the product later re-matches the rules, it re-enters the collection and its pin is restored.

</details>

## Billing & Plans

<details>
  <summary>Is there a free tier?</summary>

Yes. The Free plan allows up to 2 managed collections with no time limit and no payment required.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Is there a free trial for paid plans?</summary>

Yes. All paid plans include a 14-day free trial. You are not charged until the trial ends.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>What happens to my collections if I cancel?</summary>

Your Shopify collections are not deleted. Smart Collections stops managing them, and the products and sort order last applied by Smart Collections remain in Shopify. You regain full manual control of each collection in Shopify admin. Your Smart Collections configuration and sync history remain accessible while the app stays installed.

If you also **uninstall** the app, all Smart Collections data — sync history, rules, sort settings — is permanently deleted. This cannot be undone.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>What happens when I reach my plan's collection limit?</summary>

Smart Collections continues to sync all collections currently under management. You will see a prompt to upgrade when you try to add an additional collection. Existing collections are never paused or disrupted.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Can I downgrade my plan?</summary>

Yes. If you downgrade to a plan with a lower collection limit, your existing managed collections continue to sync. You will not be able to add new collections until your active count is within the new plan's limit.

</details>
